LinkedIn Ghostwriting: What It Actually Is, and Whether You Need It

May 31, 20266 min readBoldface

What ghostwriting actually means

LinkedIn ghostwriting is often misunderstood. People imagine someone inventing opinions and putting words in a founder's mouth. Done badly, that is exactly what it is, and it always shows. Done well, it is the opposite. Good ghostwriting is a process for extracting what a busy person already thinks and turning it into writing they would have produced themselves if they had the time and the craft. The thinking is theirs. The execution is the writer's.

How the process works

A real ghostwriting engagement starts with voice, not output. The first couple of weeks are spent understanding how the person thinks and speaks, what they believe, what they have lived through and what they actually want to be known for. From there the writer produces drafts, the client reacts, and the voice gets calibrated until a post reads as unmistakably theirs. Once that is locked, the ongoing work is a rhythm. The client supplies raw thinking, often through quick voice notes, and the writer shapes it into finished content.

Why people use it

The honest reason is simple. The people who most benefit from a strong LinkedIn presence are usually the people with the least time to build one. A founder running a company or an executive with a full calendar has the experience and the point of view that makes great content, but not the hours to write consistently, and consistency is the entire game. Ghostwriting solves the time problem without sacrificing the authenticity, as long as the voice is genuinely captured.

Who actually needs it

Not everyone does. If you enjoy writing, have the time and are already posting consistently with results, you may not need a ghostwriter at all. Ghostwriting makes sense when three things are true. You have something genuinely worth saying, you do not have the time or inclination to write it consistently yourself, and the visibility would create real value for your business or career. When those line up, it is one of the highest-leverage things a busy leader can do.

How to tell good from bad

Bad ghostwriting sounds like everyone else. It is recycled advice, safe opinions and recycled frameworks that could be attached to any name. Good ghostwriting sounds like you on your most articulate day. The test is whether someone who knows you reads a post and hears your voice. If they do, it works. If they do not, it is just content with your name on it, and people can feel the difference.
