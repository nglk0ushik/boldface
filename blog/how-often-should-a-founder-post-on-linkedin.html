How Often Should a Founder Post on LinkedIn?

May 31, 20265 min readBoldface

The question everyone asks first

When a founder decides to take LinkedIn seriously, the first question is almost always about frequency. Should I post every day? Three times a week? The question feels practical, but it is the wrong place to start, because frequency on its own determines almost nothing about whether the work succeeds.

Why frequency is the wrong frame

A founder who posts every day with nothing to say will lose to one who posts twice a week with a real point of view. The algorithm rewards engagement, and engagement comes from content people actually want to read, not from sheer volume. Posting daily out of obligation usually leads to thinner and thinner content, and the audience disengages. The goal was never to post a lot. It was to be worth following.

A realistic answer

If you want a number, two to three times a week is the sweet spot for most founders. It is frequent enough to stay present and build momentum, and infrequent enough that you can make each post genuinely good. More than that is fine if you have the substance to sustain it. Less than that makes it hard to build compounding momentum because the gaps let the audience forget you. But treat this as a floor and a ceiling to think within, not a rule to obey.

Consistency beats intensity

The single most important factor is not how often you post in a good week. It is whether you keep posting across many weeks. The founders who win are the ones still publishing in month nine, not the ones who posted daily for three weeks and burned out. A sustainable two posts a week for a year will beat an ambitious daily schedule that collapses in February every single time.

What to do instead of counting posts

Shift the question from how often to how good and how sustainable. Pick a cadence you can hold even in a busy month. Protect the quality of each post over the quantity. And if your own time is the bottleneck, which it usually is, that is exactly the problem a good content process or a ghostwriter solves. The frequency takes care of itself once the system is built. The substance is the part that has to be real.
